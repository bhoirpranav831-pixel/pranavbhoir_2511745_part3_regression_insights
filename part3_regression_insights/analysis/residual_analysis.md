5. Explain what these residuals may mean in business terms.

residuals represent difference between actual monthly sales and sales predicted by the regression model

positive residuals : arise from the fact that actual sales are higher than forecasted. This means that the store did better than anticipated. Some of the possible explanations for this are successful marketing campaigns by the store in the locality, customer loyalty, seasonality, or some other business variable not considered in the regression

negative residuals : will be observed where actual sales have been less than estimated sales. It means that the shop has underperformed against expectations. The reasons for underperformance may be more competition, problems at the store level, lack of inventory, reduced demand from customers, among others

existence of positive as well as negative residuals indicates that, although the regression model can explain much of the variations in monthly sales (R square = 0.811), there may still be certain variables influencing sales that have not been considered by the model. Hence, the model should be used to assist decision-making, but should not be the sole criteria for decisions

over-predicting stores: if the store has a negative residual it implies that the sales for the store are lower than the forecasted sales it implies that these stores are underperforming reasons may be related to inefficiency of operations high competition from other stores, low consumer demand, or lack of inventory.
